Xinchen Yu

I am an Assistant Professor of Practice in the Computer Science Department at University of Arizona.

I received my Ph.D. from University of North Texas in 2023. Before that, I earned a Master’s degree from University of Pittsburgh and a Bachelor’s degree from Zhejiang University.

My research interests lie at the intersection of natural language processing (NLP) and Computational Social Science (CSS). I am passionate about conversational behaviors and anti-social computing. Specifically, my studies focus on modeling conversations from user-generated content to explain, predict, and prevent online incivility.
